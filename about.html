About Rua

The sheer truth is that Rua was a terribly inconstant yoga practitioner. And student.

She didn’t go do yoga because she had back aches. Her back was just fine.

She didn’t go do yoga for the spiritual side of it because she thought that that was ludicrous.

She didn’t go do yoga to be surrounded by hipsters, because she can’t stand them. Even though she is one.

She stumbled upon yoga thanks to this dude she was frantically dating and because she discovered she couldn’t cross her legs.

Seriously. She really couldn’t.

She had tried getting properly into yoga time and time again over the past decade or so but it wouldn’t stick. She had a bit of a problem with hearing about crystals and full moons, she’s rather skeptical. Side effect of studying too much maths for her own good.

She’ll never be the one to do 10 hours a day although she’s met a few. And she’ll always combine within her approach a sound scientific approach. She just can’t be convinced otherwise, and it’s the kind of convection she needs to refresh regularly.

She has though been doing it more or less regularly through the past 2 years, and has in the meantime garnered a series of certifications to trick people into giving her some money for not too much work.

While in India, although she still sucked at crossing her legs many people seemed to really love her classes, including her outstanding gurus, the pranayama and the asana teacher.

This site is an attempt for her to finally commit and get better as a teacher. She’ll be trying really hard. She promises.
